Cast Target: Three Approaches

How the device target + config lives in the Media app

A

Target Bar

A persistent strip below the app header. Shows the active device name, shader pill, and volume. Tap to expand inline and change settings. Collapsed when no target is set — just shows a "Cast to..." prompt.

📡 Living Room TV 🌙 night 🔊 40 tap to change ▾

Pros

  • Always visible — zero ambiguity about where content goes
  • Expand/collapse keeps it compact
  • Natural reading order: target → content → cast

Cons

  • Burns vertical space on every screen
  • Could feel heavy with only 2 devices
B

Floating Chip

A compact chip anchored to a corner (like a FAB). Shows device icon + name. Tap opens a bottom sheet with device picker + shader/volume config. When no target, shows a generic cast icon.

📺 Living Room 🌙 40

Pros

  • Minimal footprint — doesn't eat into content area
  • Familiar pattern (Chromecast, AirPlay)
  • Bottom sheet gives room for future device types

Cons

  • Can overlap content at the edges
  • Less discoverable for first-time use
C

Header Integration

The device target lives inside the existing Media app header — a compact icon+name next to the search bar. Tap opens a popover with device list + settings. No target = dimmed cast icon.

📺 Living Rm

Pros

  • Zero extra vertical space
  • Integrates into existing layout
  • Clean — device is context, not a feature

Cons

  • Header gets crowded with more features
  • Popover has less room than a sheet for complex device configs

My recommendation: B (Floating Chip)

It's the most scalable. As you add audio-only devices, mobile targets, and kiosks, a bottom sheet can adapt its config fields per device type without cramming everything into a header or bar. The chip pattern is also universally understood from Chromecast/AirPlay. And when no target is set, it's just a small icon — zero noise.
